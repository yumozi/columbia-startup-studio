🧭 Brand Position: Priority Fields 🧭 

Brand Thesis
Why does your product exist? 
Most social media "saves" die in a digital graveyard, leaving users with a gap between what they intended to do and how they actually spend their time. Our app transforms passive, chaotic inspiration into a structured, taste-powered engine that resurfaces the right experiences at the right moment.

Target Identity
Who is this for, in their words? 
"The Intentional Explorer." You’re the person with 200+ saved Instagram posts of NYC restaurants and exhibits you swear you'll visit. You value social connection and hate the "logistical dread" of scrolling endlessly for a plan on a Friday night. You want to feel curious and in control of your leisure time rather than overwhelmed by the noise of "must-try" reels.

Canonical Language
5-10 phrases from your interviews to use everywhere:
"Saves Graveyard" – Referring to the place where TikTok/IG reels go to be forgotten.
"Taste Fingerprint" – Your unique profile of what xcites you.
"Anticipation Score" – Ranking how much you want to do something before you go.
"Low-Friction Ingestion" – Making it effortless to move a link into the app.
"Aligned Options" – Seeing experiences that match your current vibe and constraints.
"Intentional Enjoyment" – Moving from passive scrolling to active doing.

Language to Avoid
Founder-speak, jargon, or anything that doesn't sound like your users:
"Social Network" – Users like Mia and Elad expressed burnout with social features; emphasize personal utility instead.
"Productivity Planner" – Don't use words like "efficiency" or "tasks"; it makes fun planning feel like work.
"Algorithmically Curated" – Sounds cold; use "Taste-Powered" or "Personalized" to keep it human.
"Group Scheduling Tool" – Avoid focusing on the "back-and-forth" of group chats; focus on the inspiration first

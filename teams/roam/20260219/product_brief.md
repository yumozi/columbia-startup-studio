Document Goal
This document is a starting point for discussion on Roam’s initial MVP. It outlines observed problems, potential solutions, and a wedge strategy to test quickly. This is not intended to be a final strategy or perfect vision, but merely a start which we should all be encouraged to revise heavily based on user interviews and feedback.
What is Roam’s Purpose
Roam helps time-constrained and disorganized people quickly decide, commit, and execute on activities they actually want to do.
Problems and Solutions
These are the problems observed from many rounds of customer interviews along with solution hypotheses to solve these problems. Problem 1 in particular is the central MVP target wedge of this document as I believe it is the easiest to solve and test rapidly while being a core problem for the demographic of disorganized students under heavy load like engineering students or pre-med. Problem 2 is another notable and possibly also critical requirement for building Roam’s MVP, but afterthought I believe a focus on Problem 1 is a better start to this project. See Appendix for further details.
Problems
Anxiety from unclear time commitments (MVP Target Wedge)
When overwhelmed with work, it’s hard to know how long activities will take, which creates stress about whether it’s okay to have fun.


Scheduling friction (Secondary MVP Target)
Picking dates and times and committing to them requires back-and-forth communication. It also feels like scheduling fun which people find restricting


Doomscrolling and choice overload
Too many options and unstructured feeds make it difficult to decide what to do.


Forgetting saved ideas from social media
Having to revisit Instagram or TikTok to relocate ideas creates friction and lost time.


Difficulty finding relevant content
Searching manually for things you might enjoy takes effort and slows decision-making.


Coordination friction between users
Inviting others and aligning plans can be cumbersome.


Forgetting past activities
It’s hard to remembe you’ve already done, which can lead to repetition or a lack of novelty which people seem to enjoy
Solutions
Time estimates for activities (MVP Target Wedge)
Show clear time expectations with a buffer to reduce anxiety and make it easier to decide whether to engage now.


Calendar integration with suggested dates/times (Secondary MVP Target)
Offer smart scheduling suggestions to reduce back-and-forth and increase commitment


Ranking system
Prioritize and organize options to reduce overwhelm and prevent doomscrolling.


Reel ingestion system
Automatically capture and store ideas from Instagram and TikTok so users don’t have to search again.


Personalized “For You” page
Curate relevant content automatically to eliminate manual searching.


User invitation functionality
Enable easy invites to reduce coordination effort.
.
“Already Done” page
Provide a history view to help users remember past activities and avoid redundancy.


WHY NOW:
The demand is currently evolving where more and more people are trying to gain some meaningful world experiences from the events and places they like. This has partly due to fomo, and the fact that summer break and graduation is coming and that would lead to long periods without being with close friends.

# V3 Evaluation Summary — Roam Landing Page Copy

**Date:** February 2026  
**Audience:** 20 personas (4 per bucket × 5 buckets)  
**Comparison:** V2 → V3

---

## Top-Line Metrics

| Metric | V2 | V3 | Delta |
|--------|-----|-----|-------|
| Resonance (agree+) | 55% | 60% | **+5pp** |
| Intent (agree+) | 45% | 52% | **+7pp** |
| Conversion confidence (agree+) | 42% | 50% | **+8pp** |
| Dealbreaker rate | 25% | 20% | **-5pp** |
| Clarity: nailed_it | 60% | 65% | **+5pp** |

Conversion reaches 50% (target). Concrete example and “3–5 things this weekend” improved intent and conversion; dealbreakers continued to drop.

## Strongest Lines

1. **"Your saved reels don't have to die in a graveyard."** — still #1  
2. **"Example: you pick food, 2 hours, this weekend — we return 3 options from your saves and our catalog. You choose; we don't push."** — quiet_explorer, saves_graveyard  
3. **"3–5 things to do this weekend"** — routine_breaker, group_coordinator  
4. **"You have an 'actually do it' problem, not a discovery problem."** — routine_breaker  

---

## Most Common Objections

1. **"Need to see the app"** — remaining holdouts  
2. **"No group planning yet"** — group_coordinator (roadmap helps but doesn’t remove)  
3. **"Want to try before committing"** — fair; waitlist is the bridge  

---

## Recommendations for V4

- Lightly tighten hero: keep “3–5 things this weekend” prominent.
- Add one trust line (e.g. “We’re not selling your data — we use it only to build your shortlist”) if quiet_explorer still hesitates.
- Consider A/B on CTA: “Join the waitlist” vs “Get 3 ideas for this weekend.”

Brand Position Template

Team Name: Roam
Date: February 22, 2026

Purpose: This is an internal document. It captures your brand's thesis, identity, and canonical language so that everything you build — landing page, copy, features, pitch — speaks with one voice. This is not marketing copy. It is a decision reference.

Brand Thesis

The things you save shouldn’t die in a graveyard — they should turn into real experiences, without the friction that usually kills them.

Core Product Definition

Roam helps curious, overwhelmed people turn saved ideas into actual plans by resurfacing the right options at the right time based on their taste, availability, and constraints.

Existence Statement

Roam exists to close the gap between inspiration and action, so people stop defaulting to convenience and start actually doing the things they care about.

Target Identity

Roam is for someone who has a “saves graveyard,” wants to do more with their time, and is tired of the friction it takes to turn “wes” into something real.

More concretely:

They:

Have 100–300+ saved posts across Instagram, TikTok, Notes

Genuinely want to try new things

Feel the gap between intention and reality

Don’t want planning to feel like work

They are not trying to be hyper-optimized — they just want to follow through more often.

Canonical Language

“Saves Graveyard”

“Turn saves into plans”

“Aligned options”

“Right time, not just the right idea”

“From ‘we should’ to ‘we’re going’”

“Less friction, more follow-through”

Language to Avoid

Avoid: “Social network” — Because: users don’t want another platform to maintain; Roam should feel like a personal utility.

Avoid: “Productivity planner” — Because: it makes fun feel like work and creates resistance.

Avoid: “Endless options” — Because: the core pain is too many ideas already; we emphasize fewer, better options.

Avoid: “AI-powered discovery platform” — Because: this sounds abstract and cold; the value is in solving inspiration → action, not product-as-admin.

Tone Guidelines

Practical, not aspirational fluff — Grounded in real behavior and friction.

Lightweight, not heavy — Should feel easy, not like a system to maintain.

Human, not technical — Explain outcomes, not algorithms.

Helpful, not pushy — Reduce effort without forcing behavior.

Positioning Statement

For people with too many saved ideas and not enough follow-through, Roam is the product that helps them actually do the things they already want to do because it connects their ideas to the moments they can act on them.

Brand Values

Follow-through over discovery — We are not here to help users find more things. We are here to help them actually do things.

Reduce friction, don’t add structure — If something feels like work, it’s wrong. Roam should lower activation energy, not increase it.

Personal utility first — The product must be valuable alone before it becomes valuable socially.

Objection Handling

| Objection | Your Response |
|-----------|---------------|
| “I already save things everywhere.” | That’s exactly the problem — your ideas are scattered and never turn into action. Roam helps you actually follow through. |
| “I don’t want another app that feels like work.” | Roam removes work. It connects what you’ve already saved to when you can actually do it — no extra effort required. |
| “Do my friends need to be on it?” | No. It works on your own first. Sharing or coordinating is optional, not required. |
| “Isn’t this just another discovery app?” | No — discovery isn’t the problem. Execution is. Roam is built to help you act, not scroll. |
Key Interview Language

“The ideas are there. The desire is there. It’s the logistics that murder everything.”

“It’s like a graveyard of good intentions.”

“I have to be the human glue holding it all together.”

“If you don’t force people, nothing happens.”

“If it doesn’t happen, I just move on.” (low-pain segment — important contrast)

Strategic Interpretation

Roam is not a “discovery” product — people already have enough ideas.
What they lack is a low-friction way to act on them.

Implications:

Discovery is secondary

Timing > volume

Execution > inspiration

Friction reduction > feature expansion

One-Line Brand Gut Check

If it doesn’t help someone go from “I saved this” or "I want to plan this with friends/by myself" → “I actually did this,” it’s not Roam.

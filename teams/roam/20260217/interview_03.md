WARM-UP
Interviewer: “Thanks so much for taking the time to chat today.”
“Yeah totally, this sounded interesting so I figured why not.”

CONTEXT / SCREENING
“What does a typical week look like for you outside of school?”
“Uh, so I’m a CS major, pretty busy during the week but not like drowning. I have classes, I go on runs sometimes, and I usually just hang out with my roommates. We all live together so it’s super easy to just be like ‘what are you doing tonight?’ and then we’ll grab food or watch something.”
“Is there a gap between what you want to do and what you’re actually doing?”
“Not really. I feel like I do enough. I’m not someone who needs to go to a bunch of new places all the time. If something comes up, I’ll go, but I’m also fine just chilling.”

PROBLEM DISCOVERY
“Last time you saw something online and wanted to do it — what happened?”
“Honestly I don’t really save stuff like that. If I see something cool, I might send it to the group chat, but if it doesn’t happen, it’s whatever. I don’t really think about it again.”
“Have you ever used any apps for finding or saving places?”
“Yeah I used Beli for a bit. My friend told me to get it.”
“How was that experience?”
“It was fine at first, but I deleted it. None of my close friends were on it, so it felt kind of pointless. I don’t really care about tracking places if I’m not sharing it with people I actually hang out with.”
“Walk me through the last time you made plans with friends.”
“It’s usually super casual. Like someone will just say ‘let’s go get food’ and we’ll go. If it’s something bigger, like a birthday, then yeah there’s a group chat, but even then it’s not that deep. If it works, it works. If it doesn’t, we just do something else.”
“How often do plans fall through?”
“Sometimes, but I don’t really mind. If someone cancels, I’ll just stay in or do something else. It’s not a big deal.”
“What tools are you using to coordinate?”
“Just iMessage group chats. That’s it. Maybe Instagram DMs sometimes.”
“What do you like about that?”
“It’s easy. Everyone’s already there. No extra apps.”
“What feels broken about it?”
“Honestly… nothing really. It can be messy sometimes, but it’s not enough of a problem for me to want to fix it.”

DEPTH QUESTIONS
“If you could change anything about how you make plans, what would it be?”
“I don’t think I’d change much. Maybe just faster replies in group chats, but that’s about it. I wouldn’t download something just for that.”
“What would make you try a new app for planning or finding things to do?”
“It would have to be something all my friends are already using. Otherwise I won’t bother. I don’t want to be the one convincing everyone to join something.”
“So having your friends on it matters a lot?”
“Yeah, that’s the whole point. If it’s just me, there’s no reason to use it.”

WRAP-UP
“Anything I should’ve asked?”
“Maybe just that I think this kind of thing depends on the person. Like I’m pretty low-effort with plans. But I know people who get stressed about it and want everything organized. They’d probably be more helpful.”

WARM-UP
Interviewer: "Thanks so much for taking the time..."
"Yeah of course! Happy to help."

CONTEXT / SCREENING
"Can you tell me a little about yourself? What does a typical week look like outside of school?"
"So I'm a junior at Barnard, art history major, minor in econ. During the week I'm pretty locked in — classes, library, maybe the gym. But on weekends I really want to be out doing stuff. Like going to galleries, trying new restaurants, seeing friends. The problem is by the time Friday hits I'm so tired I just default to whatever's easiest, which is usually dinner at the same place near campus or just staying in."
"How would you describe your social life right now — is there a gap between what you want to do and what actually happens?"
"Oh, huge gap. I have like 200 saved posts on Instagram of places I want to go. Restaurants in the West Village, that Van Gogh immersive thing — wait, I think that closed. That's kind of the problem. I save things and then I forget about them, or the moment passes."

PROBLEM DISCOVERY
"Think about the last time you saw something online and thought, 'I want to do that.' What happened after that?"
"Okay, so like two weeks ago I saw this TikTok of a rooftop bar in Bushwick that does these themed cocktail nights. I saved the TikTok. I think I also screenshotted the name and it's somewhere in my camera roll. I texted my friend Priya like 'we HAVE to go.' She hearted the message. And then... nothing. Neither of us followed up. It's still just sitting in my saved folder."
"Why do you think it didn't happen?"
"Honestly? Nobody took the next step. Like, no one said 'okay, Saturday at 8.' And I didn't want to be the one to push it because then I have to check with everyone's schedules, and it becomes this whole thing. I just didn't have the energy."
"Walk me through the last time you actually tried to coordinate plans with friends."
"Okay, so my friend's birthday was a few weeks ago. We wanted to do dinner somewhere nice downtown. There were like seven of us in a group chat. Someone suggested a restaurant, someone else said they couldn't do Saturday, someone else said they had a budget issue. It took FOUR DAYS to pick a place and a time. By the end I was like, I don't even care where we go, I just want it to be decided. And I'm usually the one who ends up making the reservation because if I don't, nobody will."
"How often do plans fall through or saved ideas go unused?"
"Plans fall through maybe... one out of every three times? Saved ideas — honestly 90% of them just die. I never look at my saved posts. It's like a graveyard of good intentions."
"How does that feel?"
"It's honestly kind of sad. Like I feel like I'm wasting my time in New York. I moved here to have experiences and I spend most weekends doing nothing. And then Sunday night I'm like, what did I even do this weekend?"
"What are you currently using to keep track of stuff?"
"Instagram saves, screenshots, Notes app sometimes. For coordinating it's all group chats on iMessage. I use Google Calendar for classes and work shifts but not really for social stuff. Social things feel too fluid to put on a calendar, you know?"
"Have you tried anything purpose-built for this?"
"I used Partiful once for a party invite, which was fine, but it doesn't really help with the 'I have 50 ideas and no plan' problem. I tried putting fun stuff in my Google Calendar but it felt weird — like I was scheduling joy, which is kind of depressing."

DEPTH QUESTIONS
"If nothing changed for the next year, what would that feel like?"
"I'd feel like I wasted senior year honestly. I'd graduate and be like, I lived in New York City and all I did was go to Sweetgreen and the library."
"Who's usually the organizer in your friend group?"
"Me. It's always me. And I'm tired of it. Sometimes I just don't plan anything to see if someone else will, and then nobody does, and then I feel lonely. It's a lose-lose."

WRAP-UP
"Anything I should've asked?"
"Maybe about the guilt? Like I feel genuinely guilty when I don't follow through on things. It's not just inconvenience, it's like... I feel like I'm letting myself down."

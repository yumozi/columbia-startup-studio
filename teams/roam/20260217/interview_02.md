WARM-UP
Interviewer: "Thanks so much for taking the time..."
"Yeah totally, this sounded interesting so I figured why not."

CONTEXT / SCREENING
"What does a typical week look like for you outside of school?"
"Uh, so I'm a CS major so my weeks are pretty intense. I usually have assignments due like every other day. Outside of that I work out, I watch a lot of stuff on TikTok, and I try to see my friends at least once on the weekend. But 'try' is the key word."
"Is there a gap between what you want to do and what you're actually doing?"
"Yeah for sure. I'm always saying I want to go to more concerts, try new food spots, go on walks on the West Side — my friend and I literally made a list in our Notes app of restaurants we want to try. We made it in September. It's like twelve places long. We've been to one."

PROBLEM DISCOVERY
"Last time you saw something online and wanted to do it — what happened?"
"Okay so I saw a post about this dumpling spot in Chinatown, like a hidden basement place. I sent it to my friend Tess in our chat. She was like 'omg yes.' I was like 'this weekend?' She was like 'maybe, I have a paper.' And then the weekend came and she had her paper and I had a problem set and we just didn't go. Classic."
"Where'd you save it?"
"I think I just bookmarked the TikTok. I also might have sent it to myself on Slack, which sounds insane, but I do that sometimes. My storage system is an actual mess — things are spread across like five apps."
"Walk me through the last time you tried to plan something with friends."
"So a couple weeks ago I wanted to do something for my friend's 21st. I made a group chat with six people. Immediately it was chaos. Half the people didn't respond for a full day. One person could only do Friday, two could only do Saturday. Someone suggested a bar, someone else said it was too expensive. I ended up just picking something and saying 'this is what we're doing, Saturday at 9' because otherwise it would've gone on forever. And even then, two people bailed day-of."
"How often does this kind of thing happen?"
"The bailing? Pretty often. I'd say like 40% of the time at least one person drops. And the whole planning-by-committee thing happens every single time. It's exhausting. I've started just making decisions unilaterally because the group consensus thing never works."
"What tools are you using?"
"Group chats for coordination. Notes app for my restaurant list. TikTok saves and Instagram saves for random ideas. Google Calendar for my actual schedule but I've never put a social thing on it. Oh, and sometimes I use When2Meet for bigger stuff, but nobody fills it out on time."
"What do you like about your current setup?"
"Honestly... nothing? It's all duct tape. The group chat works because everyone's already there, but it's horrible for actually making decisions. And my saved stuff is just a black hole."
"What feels broken?"
"Everything is fragmented. My ideas are in one place, my friends are in another place, my calendar is in another place, and none of them talk to each other. So I have to be the human glue holding it all together, and most of the time I just don't bother."

DEPTH QUESTIONS
"If you could wave a magic wand and fix this, what would change?"
"I think I'd want something that takes all the stuff I've saved — restaurants, events, whatever — and somehow connects it to when my friends are actually free. Like, don't make me do the work of cross-referencing my ideas with everyone's availability. That's the part that kills it. The ideas are there. The desire is there. It's the logistics that murder everything."
"Would you want to plan ahead or have something suggest stuff in the moment?"
"Honestly both? Like during the week I'd want to casually drop things into a list. And then when the weekend is coming, I'd want it to be like 'hey, Tess is free Saturday afternoon, and you saved that dumpling spot — want to make it happen?' That would be a dream. But it can't feel pushy or productivity-bro about it. Like if it gives me a notification that says 'you're falling behind on your experience goals' I'm deleting it immediately."

WRAP-UP
"Anything I should've asked?"
"Maybe about the social dynamics? Like there's always one person in the group who's the planner. And being that person is a thankless job. If a tool could take some of that weight off the planner's shoulders, that alone would be huge. I didn't sign up to be a project manager for my friend group's social life, but here I am."
"Anyone else you'd recommend I talk to?"
"Yeah, talk to my friend Tess, she's even worse about this than I am. She has like 400 TikTok saves and has done maybe three of them. I'll text you her number."

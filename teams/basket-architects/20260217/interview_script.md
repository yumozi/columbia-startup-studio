# 1. RadiusCart (The Cheapest Grocery Bucket)

## Research Question
How do price-sensitive urbanites currently manage the trade-off between getting the lowest price and the physical effort of shopping?

## Questions

### Discover real use cases
- Tell me about your last grocery run. Walk me through it from the moment you realized you needed food to when you put the groceries away.
- You mentioned going to [Store A]. Did you consider going anywhere else? Why or why not?
- Tell me about a time in the last month when you felt you "overpaid" for something. What happened?

### Discover friction
- What is the most annoying part of deciding where to shop for groceries?
- Do you ever check prices before you leave the house? If yes, show me how you do that right now.
- Have you ever visited two different stores in one trip? Tell me about that specific day. What made you do it?

### Magic wand
- If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about how you buy your weekly staples, what would it be?

# 2. Silver Platter (The Luxury Concierge)

## Research Question
What friction exists for high-income individuals when they try to source specific, high-quality ingredients for hosting or personal consumption?

## Questions

### Discover real use cases
- Tell me about the last time you hosted a dinner or cooked a "special" meal. Where did you get the ingredients?
- Walk me through your decision process when buying [Specific Luxury Item, e.g., Steaks, Wine, Cheese]. What are the "must-haves" for you?

### Discover friction
- What is the most frustrating part of getting everything you need for a high-quality meal?
- Have you ever hired someone or used a service to help with shopping? How did that go?
- Tell me about a time you settled for an ingredient you weren't happy with because you didn't have time to go to the specialty store.

### Magic wand
- If you could have your dream kitchen infrastructure handled by someone else, what tasks would you give away immediately?

# 3. The Experience Ranker (Beli for Experiences)

## Research Question
How do users currently track and "show off" their taste in activities (beyond just food)?

## Questions

### Discover real use cases (the "flex")
- Show me your Notes app or Google Maps. Do you have any lists of "places to go" or "places I've been"? Walk me through how you organize them.
- Tell me about the last time you recommended a specific activity (not a restaurant) to a friend. How did you describe it? Did you compare it to anything?
- Think of the last "mediocre" event you went to. How did you describe it to people afterwards? What made it a 5/10 instead of a 10/10?

### Discover friction (the "fragmented memory")
- How do you currently remember which bars/clubs/pop-ups you liked and which ones you hated? Do you write it down?
- Have you ever looked back at your camera roll and forgotten where a specific photo was taken? Tell me about that.
- Do you use anything right now to track events? (e.g., Partiful, RA Guide, Dice). What do you hate about them?

### Magic wand
- If you could have a perfect "Resume" of your social life in NYC, what would be on it? (e.g., Every club entered? Every hike completed?)
